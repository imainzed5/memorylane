Simple by design.

Step 01

Runs quietly in your system tray.

No window, no interruption, and no extra workflow to maintain.

Step 02

Captures at the interval you choose.

Set it anywhere from 1 minute to 4 hours, depending on how much detail you want.

Step 03

Open it whenever you need to rewind.

Scroll back through your day capture by capture and recover the context you forgot.

Your screen stays on your machine.

MemoryLane is a local desktop tool, not a service. Captures are stored under your Windows AppData directory, and the app does not need an account, a backend, or a sync step to be useful.

You decide how long captures are kept, and you decide how much disk space they are allowed to use. If the storage cap is reached, MemoryLane deletes the oldest days first and keeps the recent history intact.

  • All screenshots and notes are written locally to AppData, not uploaded anywhere.
  • No account required, no telemetry, no cloud sync, and no internet connection in the loop.
  • Choose your retention period in days so the archive matches how far back you actually look.
  • Set a storage cap in GB and MemoryLane automatically removes the oldest days when the cap is hit.
